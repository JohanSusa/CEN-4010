Workout List

    CHEST

    CHEST FLYS

    Performed on a pec deck or chest fly machine, targets the pectoral muscles by providing a controlled and consistent resistance. To perform it, adjust the seat height so the handles are at chest level, sit with your back against the pad, and grasp the handles with a slight bend in your elbows. Push the handles together in a wide arc, squeezing your chest muscles, then slowly return to the starting position. Focus on a controlled movement, proper breathing, and using a full range of motion to maximize effectiveness and avoid injury.

    BENCH PRESS

    The bench press is a fundamental strength training exercise that targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps. To perform it, lie on a flat bench with your feet flat on the floor. Grip the barbell slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, lower it to your chest, and then press it back up until your arms are fully extended. Focus on maintaining a controlled movement, keeping your back flat on the bench, and exhaling as you press the weight up. The bench press is essential for building upper body strength and muscle mass.

    INCLINE CHEST PRESS

    The incline bench press targets the upper chest, shoulders, and triceps. To perform it, set an adjustable bench to a 30-45 degree incline. Lie back with your feet flat on the floor and grip the barbell slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower the barbell to your upper chest, then press it back up until your arms are fully extended. Focus on a controlled movement, keeping your back pressed against the bench, and exhaling as you lift the weight. This exercise helps build upper chest strength and definition.

    SHOULDER

    SHOULDER PRESS

    The shoulder press machine targets the deltoid muscles, triceps, and upper chest. To perform it, adjust the seat so that the handles are at shoulder height when you sit down. Sit with your back firmly against the pad and grip the handles. Press the handles upward until your arms are fully extended, then slowly lower them back to the starting position. Focus on maintaining a controlled movement, keeping your core engaged, and exhaling as you press the weight up. This machine provides a stable and safe way to build shoulder strength and muscle mass.

    LATERAL RAISES

    Dumbbell lateral raises target the lateral deltoid muscles, enhancing shoulder width and definition. To perform them, stand with feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at your sides. With a slight bend in your elbows, raise the dumbbells out to the sides until your arms are parallel to the floor. Slowly lower them back to the starting position. Focus on a controlled movement, keeping your core engaged, and avoiding momentum. Exhale as you lift the weights. This exercise effectively isolates the shoulder muscles, contributing to overall shoulder development.

    REAR DELTS

    The rear delts machine targets the rear deltoid muscles, upper back, and rhomboids. To perform it, adjust the seat and handles so that your arms are parallel to the floor when you sit down. Sit with your chest against the pad, grip the handles, and pull them back in a wide arc until your arms are extended straight out to your sides. Slowly return to the starting position. Focus on a controlled movement, keeping your back straight and squeezing your shoulder blades together. This exercise helps build upper back strength and improves shoulder stability.

    BACK

    LAT PULLDOWN

    The lat pulldown targets the latissimus dorsi muscles, aiding in back width and overall upper body strength. To perform it, sit at the lat pulldown machine with your thighs secured under the pad. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, palms facing forward. Pull the bar down to your upper chest while keeping your back straight and elbows pointed down. Slowly return the bar to the starting position. Focus on a controlled movement, engaging your back muscles, and exhaling as you pull the bar down. This exercise effectively strengthens the upper back and improves posture.

    CABLE ROWS

    Cable rows are a compound exercise that primarily targets the middle back, biceps, and rear delts. To perform cable rows, sit at a cable row machine with your feet securely planted and knees slightly bent. Grab the handles with an overhand grip, keeping your back straight and shoulders relaxed. Pull the handles towards your abdomen, squeezing your shoulder blades together at the peak of the movement. Slowly release back to the starting position, maintaining control throughout. Focus on a smooth, controlled motion and exhale as you pull the handles towards you. Cable rows are effective for building back strength, improving posture, and enhancing overall upper body stability.

    MACHINE ROWS

    Machine rows are a strength training exercise that targets the middle back, biceps, and rear delts. To perform machine rows, sit on the machine with your chest against the pad and feet firmly on the platform. Grasp the handles with an overhand grip, keeping your back straight and shoulders relaxed. Pull the handles towards your abdomen, squeezing your shoulder blades together at the peak of the movement. Slowly release back to the starting position, maintaining control throughout. Focus on a smooth, controlled motion and exhale as you pull the handles towards you. Machine rows are beneficial for building back strength, improving posture, and enhancing overall upper body stability.

    TRICEP

    SKULL CRUSHER

    Skull crushers, also known as lying triceps extensions, target the triceps muscles effectively. To perform them, lie flat on a bench with a barbell or dumbbells held above your chest, arms extended. Lower the weight towards your forehead by bending your elbows, then extend your arms to raise it back to the starting position. Focus on keeping your upper arms stationary and elbows pointed upward throughout the exercise. Exhale as you push the weight back up. Skull crushers are ideal for developing triceps strength and size, but proper form is crucial to avoid strain on the elbows or wrists.

    SEATED DIP MACHINE

    The seated dip machine targets the triceps, chest, and shoulders. To perform it, adjust the seat height so that when seated, your arms are bent at a 90-degree angle when gripping the handles. Keep your back straight against the pad and feet flat on the floor. Push down on the handles to extend your arms fully, then slowly return to the starting position. Focus on a controlled movement, exhaling as you push down, and keeping your elbows close to your sides. This exercise helps build upper body strength and can be adjusted for different levels of resistance to suit varying fitness levels.

    TRICEP PUSHDOWN

    Tricep pushdowns are a popular isolation exercise that targets the triceps. To perform them, stand facing a cable machine with a high pulley attachment and grab the straight bar or rope with an overhand grip. Position your elbows close to your sides and extend your arms downwards, pushing the bar or rope until your arms are fully extended. Keep your upper arms stationary throughout the movement, focusing on squeezing your triceps at the bottom of the movement. Slowly return to the starting position, maintaining control. Exhale as you push down and inhale as you return. Tricep pushdowns are effective for developing triceps strength and definition, contributing to overall arm aesthetics and strength.

    BICEP

    HAMMER CURLS

    Hammer curls target the brachialis and brachioradialis muscles of the forearm, as well as the biceps. To perform them, stand or sit with a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing your body (neutral grip). Keep your elbows close to your sides and curl the dumbbells upward, stopping just short of your shoulders. Hold for a moment at the top, then lower the dumbbells back down in a controlled manner. Focus on keeping your wrists neutral and avoiding swinging your arms. Exhale as you lift the weights. Hammer curls are effective for building forearm and bicep strength, improving grip strength, and adding definition to the arms.

    PREACHER CURLS

    Preacher curls target the biceps muscles, emphasizing the peak contraction. To perform them, sit at a preacher curl bench with your upper arms resting on the pad and your chest against it. Grip a barbell or dumbbells with an underhand grip, allowing your arms to fully extend. Curl the weight upwards while keeping your upper arms firmly on the pad. Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then lower the weight back down under control. Focus on maintaining strict form, keeping your elbows stable, and exhaling as you lift the weight. Preacher curls are effective for isolating the biceps, enhancing muscle definition, and improving arm strength.

    BICEP CURLS

    Bicep curls are a fundamental exercise targeting the biceps brachii muscles. To perform them, stand or sit with a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing forward. Keep your elbows close to your sides and curl the dumbbells upward towards your shoulders, rotating your palms upwards as you lift. Hold briefly at the top, then lower the dumbbells back down in a controlled manner. Focus on a smooth, controlled movement, avoiding swinging or using momentum. Exhale as you lift the weights. Bicep curls are versatile and effective for building bicep strength, enhancing arm definition, and improving overall upper body aesthetics.

    LEGS

    LEG EXTENSIONS

    The leg extension machine targets the quadriceps muscles at the front of the thigh. To perform the exercise, sit on the machine with your knees bent at a 90-degree angle and your ankles positioned under the padded bar. Extend your legs upward, lifting the weight until your legs are almost straight, but avoid locking your knees. Lower the weight back down in a controlled manner to complete one repetition. Focus on maintaining a steady pace and breathing throughout the movement. Leg extensions are effective for isolating and strengthening the quadriceps, which is beneficial for improving lower body strength and muscular endurance.

    LEG CURLS

    The leg curl machine targets the hamstrings, located at the back of the thigh. To perform the exercise, lie face down on the machine with your legs straight and the back of your ankles under the padded lever. Curl your legs upwards by bending your knees until the lever touches your buttocks, then slowly lower back to the starting position. Maintain control throughout the movement to effectively engage the hamstrings. Leg curls are excellent for isolating and strengthening the hamstring muscles, which is essential for balanced lower body strength and improving athletic performance.

    SQUATS

    Squats are a foundational compound exercise that targets multiple muscle groups, primarily the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. To perform a squat, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointed slightly outward. Lower your body by bending at the knees and hips, as if you're sitting back into an imaginary chair, while keeping your chest up and back straight. Lower yourself until your thighs are parallel to the ground or slightly below, ensuring your knees do not extend beyond your toes. Push through your heels to return to the starting position. Focus on maintaining proper form, such as keeping your knees in line with your toes and not letting them collapse inward. Squats are effective for building lower body strength, improving overall muscle tone, and enhancing functional fitness.
